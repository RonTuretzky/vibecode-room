Tiered vocabulary: always-hot vs. state-gated

D-DD-24   Panopticon V0 — final pass  ·  docs/planning/02-design.md §5.1

The puzzle

The command set lists "Yes" / "Accept" as the acceptance words — yet design-art.md §4 bans common conversational words ("yes", "go", "start") as magic words. Both cannot be right unless we distinguish when a word is live.

Decision

Split the vocabulary into two tiers, and let the collision-resistance bar scale with the cost of a false trigger:
  • Always-hot (matched any time → must be rare/non-conversational): wake "Panop", every callsign, mute "Curtain", unmute "Daybreak", panic "Abort", stop "Halt".
  • State-gated (matched only inside a narrow window → may reuse natural words): accept/decline (only in SUGGESTION_DELIVERY), "Done"/"Back" (only in an open steering window), "Confirm" (only while a destructive read-back is pending).

Why "Yes" is safe as a state-gated accept word

"Yes" is never live except as a direct answer to a question the system just asked (the pending-suggestion window). Outside that window it is inert — a casual "yes, exactly" in conversation cannot spawn a process. The risk design-art.md warns about only applies to always-hot matching; gating removes it.

Callsigns are always-hot (clarifies the state machine)

The spine's state machine has IDLE ──[callsign]──► STEERING_WINDOW with no wake word required — so callsigns are always-hot and therefore must pass the strict collision guard (which the pre-validated NATO subset does). The wake word "Panop" gates only the general attention/status flow, not addressing an already-running process.

Alternatives

A. Make everything always-hot; ban "yes", require a coined accept word. Rejected: forces users to learn an unnatural word for the single most natural spoken response ("yes"); higher onboarding friction for no safety gain over gating.
B. Make everything state-gated. Rejected: mute/panic/callsigns must work from any state — they cannot be gated.
C. Two tiers + cost-scaled bar (chosen). Chosen.

Verification

  • Tier-gating test (unit): "Yes" outside SUGGESTION_DELIVERY is inert; "Confirm" outside a pending read-back is inert. RBG: make "Yes" always-hot → a casual "yes" spawns a process → fails.
  • Collision test: all always-hot words pairwise pass Metaphone + phoneme-Levenshtein ≤2.
  • E2e: un-addressed talk containing "yes/stop/go" never triggers a command unless the gating window is open; a near-homophone of a callsign in casual speech does not mis-select.
  • Observability: command.recognize{phrase, matchedCommand|null, tier, distanceScore}.
