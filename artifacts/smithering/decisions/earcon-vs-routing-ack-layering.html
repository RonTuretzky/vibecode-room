Two intentionally disjoint non-verbal layers

D-DD-23   Panopticon V0 — final pass  ·  docs/planning/02-design.md §3

The inconsistency in the draft

The draft said "exactly 5 earcons, no more" and then introduced routing acks ("tick-tick", "whoosh") as "functional equivalents." Read literally, that is either a 6th/7th earcon (contradiction) or an undefined category. A verifier could not tell whether an acoustic-distinctness test should treat them as earcons.

Decision

The system speaks through two categorically different non-verbal layers, and the routing acks are deliberately non-tonal so they can never collide with the tonal state earcons. "Exactly 5" governs Layer A only.
Layer A — tonal state earcons (fixed at 5)

Pitched, melodic, ≤500 ms. Signal what state the system is in.

  • E1 Wake (C5→E5) · E2 Transcribing hum (A2) · E3 Spawn (G5) · E4 Resolve (C4→E4→G4) · E5 Halt (E5→C4)
Layer B — routing acks (non-tonal)

Clicks / whoosh, no pitched content. Signal where an addressed utterance was routed. Ignored ambient speech is silent — of course un-addressed chatter should make no noise.

  • route.steer:X → double-click "tick-tick"
  • route.suggestion → soft "whoosh"
  • observe.pass / route.pass (ignored ambient) → silence (default, not a knob)

Why non-tonal is the key constraint

Making Layer B non-tonal guarantees disjointness by construction: a click can never be mistaken for a melodic earcon, and vice-versa, even under conversational noise and cognitive load (the failure mode design-art.md §2 warns about). This also preserves the ATC "Roger vs. Wilco" distinction — E1 (wake) = "received", E3 (spawn) = "acting on it" — without crowding the tonal space.

Alternatives

A. Expand to 7 tonal earcons. Rejected: more pitched signatures to keep mutually distinct; erodes the "5 is the minimum distinguishable set" discipline.
B. Drop routing acks; rely on TTS for routing. Rejected: spoken routing receipts are slow and chatty — violates the restraint/latency bar.
C. Two disjoint layers; Layer B non-tonal (chosen). Chosen.

Verification

  • Acoustic-distinctness fixture (unit): assert E1–E5 differ in pitch register and rhythm, and every Layer-B ack is non-tonal. RBG: give a routing ack a pitched tone → an overlap detector flags Layer A/B collision → fails.
  • E2e: ignored ambient observe.pass / route.pass emits silence by default — a positive assertion that no ack was played, not merely "nothing logged." This is the requirement, not a configurable knob (see ENG-A-10).
  • Observability: earcon.emit{id} (Layer A) vs. route.*{ackKind} (Layer B) are distinct event families in the trace.
