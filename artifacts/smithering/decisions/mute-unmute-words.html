Mute / unmute words: "Curtain" / "Daybreak"

D-DD-21   Panopticon V0 design — final pass  ·  closes the [mute word] placeholder  ·  docs/planning/02-design.md §2.2

The gap

The PRD and the design draft carried a [mute word] placeholder and, in the command table, the literal words "Mute" and "Listen". These are always-hot words (live at any moment), yet design-art.md §4 explicitly bans common conversational words for exactly this slot — "listen, I think…" and "can you mute that" occur constantly in team speech and would misfire.

Decision

Mute = "Curtain", Unmute = "Daybreak". Both are provisional pending P-CUE acoustic validation against the team's actual vocabulary and against "Panop"/"Abort"/all active callsigns (collision guard, Metaphone + phoneme-Levenshtein ≤2).

Selection criteria (from design-art §1/§4)

Alternatives

"Mute" / "Listen" (status quo). Rejected: both appear in ordinary speech; "Listen, …" is a near-ubiquitous discourse marker.
"Eclipse" / "Sunrise". Good paired metaphor, but "Eclipse" has a vowel onset (weaker keyword anchor than a plosive).
"Curtain" / "Daybreak" (chosen). Both plosive-leading, rare, paired. Chosen, pending the acoustic probe.
Open: word choice is principled but not final until P-CUE runs the acoustic/collision validation against the real team vocabulary. If either collides, substitute another plosive-leading rare word from the same metaphor family.

Verification

  • Unit: collision-guard test asserts "Curtain"/"Daybreak" pass vs. wake/panic/callsigns; RBG: inject "Listen" → flagged against the conversational corpus → fails.
  • E2e: speak each in isolation → documented effect; speak near-homophones in casual talk → no misfire.
  • Onboarding: the consent line now says "Say 'Curtain' to pause" (was [mute word]).
