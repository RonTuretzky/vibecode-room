Mute / unmute words: "mute" / "unmute"

D-DD-21   Vibersyn V0 design — final pass  ·  closes the [mute word] placeholder  ·  docs/planning/02-design.md §2.2

The gap

The PRD and the design draft carried a [mute word] placeholder. We need a concrete pair of words for the pause/resume control. Earlier passes worried about always-hot words misfiring on ordinary speech ("listen, I think…", "can you mute that") and reached for exotic coinages. That worry no longer applies: Cue (the voice library by Etheria) owns wake/keyword robustness, so we can use the plainest possible English words.

Decision

Mute = "mute", Unmute = "unmute". Plain English words — say "mute" to pause feeding audio into the suggestion/routing pipeline, say "unmute" to resume. The screen also always offers an "unmute" button (see D-DD-22 / ENG-A-13). Keyword robustness against the team's real vocabulary is handled by Cue, not by a bespoke collision-distance scheme.

Why plain words are fine now

The earlier rationale for exotic, phoneme-distant coinages existed only because we assumed Vibersyn had to do its own always-on keyword spotting and collision-guarding. With Cue handling wake/keyword activation, "mute"/"unmute" are the most discoverable, zero-onboarding choice. The voice library is responsible for not firing on incidental conversational uses; we trust it for that.

Alternatives

Exotic coined words ("Curtain" / "Daybreak"). Rejected: these were chosen for phoneme-distance / collision-resistance reasons that no longer apply now that Cue owns keyword robustness; they add onboarding friction for zero benefit.
"mute" / "unmute" (chosen). Plain English, instantly discoverable, robustness delegated to the voice library. Chosen.
Note: we do not run our own acoustic/collision validation against the team vocabulary. Cue handles keyword activation and false-trigger suppression; if a word ever proves problematic in the field, swap it via the env-tunable word list (ENG-A-01).

Verification

  • Integration: assert "mute"/"unmute" are wired as the mute/unmute keywords through the Cue integration; RBG: wire a different word → the control no longer toggles → fails. We verify only the wiring into Cue, not Cue's own recognition quality.
  • E2e: say "mute" → pipeline pauses; say "unmute" (or press the on-screen unmute button) → resumes.
  • Onboarding: the consent line now says "Say 'mute' to pause" (was [mute word]).
