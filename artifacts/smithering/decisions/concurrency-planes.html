Three concurrency planes: Hot · Decision · Durable

ENG-A-01   Panopticon V0 engineering  ·  the central architectural bet  ·  docs/planning/03-eng.md §0, §1

The problem

The product has an extreme latency asymmetry baked into its requirements. An earcon acknowledgement must fire in ≤300 ms and the command round-trip must be <1 s p50 (REQ-10) — while a durable process spawn may legitimately take up to 3 s (REQ-4 AC4.3) and a durable run takes minutes. If the fast ack shared a thread or an await-chain with the slow durable path, one slow Smithers spawn or one TTS render would blow the 300 ms budget and the room would perceive the system as laggy — the exact failure the PRD calls unbearable.

Decision

Split the system into three planes that communicate only through queues, so a stall in a slower plane can never delay a faster one:
PlaneBudgetContainsNever waits on
Hot≤300 msaudio in/out, ASR finalization, earcon dispatch (pre-rendered PCM)LLM · Smithers · TTS · network
Decision~100 msCue policies, cheap/fast LLM (temp-0), observe.passSmithers · durable runs
Durableseconds–minSmithers runs, spawn, steer, recovery, safety hold—
The earcon is dispatched from the Hot plane the instant ASR finalizes a recognized command, before and independent of any decision/Smithers/TTS work.

Data flow (the spine)

mic → ASR(isFinal) → CueAdapter → CuePolicy decision
   ├─ [recognized command] → EarconEngine.emit ≤300 ms   ← HOT, parallel, waits on nothing
   └─ MappedActionTool action → ActionDispatcher → Smithers (async spawn ≤3 s)
                                   → streamRunEvents → RunEventNormalizer → CueAdapter
                                   → OutputPolicy (silent | earcon | ≤15-word TTS) → speaker

Alternatives

Single async pipeline (one await-chain). Rejected: the earcon would be serialized behind whatever the decision/Smithers step costs; a 3 s spawn = a 3 s-late ack.
Two planes (fast vs slow), earcon inside the decision plane. Rejected: a slow LLM decision (even at ~100 ms p50, p99 spikes) still gates the 300 ms ack. The ack must not depend on the decision at all — only on ASR finalization.
Three planes, queue-decoupled (chosen). Chosen. The Hot plane owns only what must be instant; everything slow is downstream and asynchronous. Matches Pipecat's pre-rendered-PCM-bypasses-TTS pattern (eng-oss §1.5).

Verification

  • Unit: earcon-latency test (mocked clock) — E1 ≤300 ms after isFinal; RBG: set budget to 100 ms → fails; relax to 300 ms → passes.
  • Unit: async-dispatch test — a mocked slow Smithers spawn does not block the next observation from processing on the decision plane.
  • E2e: latency benchmark over ≥100 live round-trips — earcon <300 ms, round-trip p50<1 s / p95<1.5 s, recorded as a regression baseline that fails on regression.
Constraint it imposes: earcons must be pre-rendered PCM (no synthesis on the hot path), and the seam (ENG-A-03) must be async — spawn returns immediately and completes off the hot path. Both are load-bearing for this decision.
