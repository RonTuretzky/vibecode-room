The hot-plane earcon is triggered by Cue's deterministic TextCue

ENG-A-09REQ-10Cue constraint (D2)Resolves R4

The apparent contradiction (R4)

"The plan does not explain how the hot plane knows a command was recognized without either duplicating command recognition outside Cue or waiting on Cue."

Two requirements look opposed: (a) the standing constraint requires magic-word recognition to be built on Cue; (b) REQ-10 requires the earcon ≤300 ms, which cannot wait on the LLM-scored decision or Smithers.

The resolution — CONFIRMED: Cue does recognition

Confirmed. Cue handles keyword/intent recognition — "Cue does that." We do not build a custom recognizer. The earcon fires off Cue's own deterministic recognition outcome.

Split Cue's deterministic eval from its LLM-scored decision

Cue does two different things at different speeds:

Cue stageSpeedDrives
TextCue literal/regex match of a magic worddeterministic, in-process, no LLMthe hot-plane earcon
LLM-scored intent/quality decision (suggestion firing, etc.)~100 ms + networkthe slow routing/dispatch path

The earcon fires off the deterministic TextCue outcome that Cue already produces — so recognition is genuinely on Cue (constraint satisfied) — while the slow, LLM-scored decision proceeds independently and never blocks the earcon. The latency split is between Cue's fast and slow stages, not between a custom recognizer and Cue.

ASR isFinal ─▶ Cue Adapter ─▶ Cue TextCue (deterministic) ─┬─▶ EarconEngine.emit  ≤300 ms  (HOT)
                                                           └─▶ LLM-scored decision ─▶ dispatch (SLOW)

The one risk, and how the probe closes it

This only holds if a TextCue decision actually resolves within budget. P-CUE gets a new assertion: does a TextCue decision resolve within the earcon budget (target ≤150 ms, headroom under 300 ms)? Two outcomes:

P-CUE resultAction
TextCue resolves in budgetNo fallback. Earcon fed directly by the Cue decision object.
Cue batches decisions / cooldownSeconds integer-only → match not observable in budgetRecorded fallback (owned extension + risk, D2): an adapter pre-matcher that mirrors the exact TextCue word list/semantics solely to drive the earcon. It has no routing authority — the authoritative route still comes from Cue's decision. A config-parity test asserts the pre-matcher's word list is byte-equal to the TextCue config.

Why not the rejected options

  • Duplicate a full command recognizer outside Cue — two authorities, drift, violates the Cue constraint.
  • Wait on Cue's full LLM-scored decision — blows the 300 ms budget.

Verification

  • Unit: recognition-source test — the earcon trigger is the Cue TextCue decision (or, in fallback, a pre-matcher with a byte-equal word list); no routing decision is taken outside Cue.
  • E2e: recognition-latency e2e — earcon fires ≤300 ms after isFinal while the LLM decision is artificially delayed (proves the earcon does not wait on the slow path).
  • Probe: P-CUE TextCue-latency assertion (red-before-green).
